# VPD (Vapor Pressure Deficit) Guide

## What is VPD?

Vapor Pressure Deficit (VPD) is the difference between the amount of moisture in the air and how much moisture the air can hold when saturated. It's measured in kilopascals (kPa) and is a better indicator of plant transpiration than humidity alone.

## Why VPD Matters

**Benefits of Optimal VPD:**
- Maximizes nutrient uptake
- Prevents mold and mildew
- Reduces stress on plants
- Optimizes photosynthesis
- Improves transpiration rate
- Better stomatal regulation

## VPD Calculation

VPD = SVP(leaf) - AVP(air)

Where:
- SVP = Saturation Vapor Pressure
- AVP = Actual Vapor Pressure

**Simplified formula:**
VPD = (1 - RH/100) × SVP(temp)

## Optimal VPD Ranges by Growth Stage

### Propagation/Clones
- **VPD Range:** 0.4-0.8 kPa
- **Temperature:** 75-80°F (24-27°C)
- **Humidity:** 75-85%
- **Reasoning:** Low VPD reduces stress while roots develop

### Seedling Stage
- **VPD Range:** 0.4-0.8 kPa
- **Temperature:** 72-80°F (22-27°C)
- **Humidity:** 65-75%
- **Reasoning:** Gentle environment for root establishment

### Early Vegetative
- **VPD Range:** 0.8-1.0 kPa
- **Temperature:** 70-82°F (21-28°C)
- **Humidity:** 55-70%
- **Reasoning:** Increasing transpiration for growth

### Late Vegetative
- **VPD Range:** 1.0-1.2 kPa
- **Temperature:** 72-85°F (22-29°C)
- **Humidity:** 50-65%
- **Reasoning:** Maximum transpiration for rapid growth

### Early Flowering
- **VPD Range:** 1.0-1.3 kPa
- **Temperature:** 70-82°F (21-28°C)
- **Humidity:** 50-60%
- **Reasoning:** Balanced for bud development

### Mid Flowering
- **VPD Range:** 1.2-1.5 kPa
- **Temperature:** 68-80°F (20-27°C)
- **Humidity:** 45-55%
- **Reasoning:** Lower humidity prevents mold

### Late Flowering
- **VPD Range:** 1.2-1.5 kPa
- **Temperature:** 65-78°F (18-26°C)
- **Humidity:** 40-50%
- **Reasoning:** Dense buds need dry conditions

## Leaf Temperature Offset

Leaf surface temperature is typically 2-5°F lower than air temperature due to transpiration cooling.

**Key Points:**
- Use leaf temperature for accurate VPD if possible
- Infrared thermometers measure leaf temp
- Account for 2-3°F difference in calculations
- Higher light = greater leaf cooling

## VPD and CO2 Supplementation

When using CO2 enrichment (1000-1500 ppm):
- Plants can handle higher VPD (up to 1.6 kPa)
- Higher temperatures tolerated (up to 85-90°F)
- Stomata stay open longer
- Adjust humidity to maintain target VPD

## Environmental Control Methods

### Lowering VPD (Too High)
- Increase humidity (humidifier)
- Lower temperature
- Mist leaves (temporary)
- Reduce light intensity
- Increase watering frequency

### Raising VPD (Too Low)
- Decrease humidity (dehumidifier)
- Increase temperature
- Improve air circulation
- Increase light intensity
- Reduce watering

## Signs of VPD Problems

### VPD Too Low (<0.4 kPa)
- Slow growth
- Nutrient deficiencies
- Edema (blisters on leaves)
- Fungal diseases (powdery mildew, bud rot)
- Soft, weak stems

### VPD Too High (>1.6 kPa)
- Wilting even when watered
- Leaf curling/cupping
- Brown leaf edges
- Nutrient lockout
- Stunted growth
- Increased water consumption

## Nighttime VPD

**During dark period:**
- Temperature: 5-10°F lower than daytime
- Humidity can increase naturally
- VPD naturally drops
- Risk of mold increases in flower
- Use dehumidifier if needed

## Equipment for VPD Management

**Monitoring:**
- Digital thermometer/hygrometer
- Infrared thermometer (leaf temp)
- VPD calculator app

**Control:**
- AC/heating for temperature
- Humidifier (ultrasonic or evaporative)
- Dehumidifier
- Exhaust fan with controller
- Intake fan
- Oscillating fans

## VPD Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Slow veg growth | VPD too low | Reduce humidity or raise temp |
| Leaf taco/curl | VPD too high | Increase humidity or lower temp |
| Powdery mildew | VPD too low | Increase airflow, raise VPD |
| Bud rot in flower | VPD too low | Dehumidify, increase airflow |
| Calcium deficiency | VPD too high | Lower VPD, add cal-mag |
| Yellowing new leaves | VPD fluctuation | Stabilize environment |
